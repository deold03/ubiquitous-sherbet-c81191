Navigating Chicago's short-term rental regulations can feel overwhelming — especially if you're new to hosting or considering converting a long-term rental to an STR. This guide breaks down what you need to know.

## The Shared Housing Ordinance

Chicago regulates short-term rentals under its Shared Housing Ordinance, which went into effect in 2016 and has been updated several times since. The key takeaway: you need a license before you list.

### License Types

There are two main license categories:

**Shared Housing Unit License** — For hosts renting out their primary residence (or a unit in a building with 3 or fewer units). This is the most common path for individual hosts.

**Vacation Rental License** — For hosts renting a unit that is *not* their primary residence, or in a larger building.

## Zoning Restrictions

Not every property in Chicago can legally operate as a short-term rental. Before purchasing a property for STR use, verify its zoning classification.

Key restrictions include:
- Many residential zones require the unit to be owner-occupied
- Certain aldermanic wards have enacted local restrictions beyond the citywide ordinance
- Condo and co-op buildings often have their own rules that supersede city licensing

## The Application Process

Applying for a license involves:

1. Submitting an application through the City of Chicago Business Portal
2. Providing proof of insurance (minimum $1M liability)
3. Completing a safety inspection (smoke detectors, carbon monoxide detectors, emergency egress)
4. Paying the annual license fee

The process typically takes 4–8 weeks. Budget accordingly before your first booking.

## Staying Compliant

Once licensed, you have ongoing obligations:
- Display your license number on all listing platforms
- Remit Chicago's 4.5% Hotel Accommodation Tax and applicable state taxes
- Maintain records of all stays
- Renew your license annually
