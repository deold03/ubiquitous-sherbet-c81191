One of the most common questions we hear from new hosts: "Should I list on Airbnb, VRBO, or Booking.com?" 

The short answer: all three. But the longer answer explains *how* to think about each platform — and why your revenue mix across platforms matters more than which one you choose.

## Platform Profiles

### Airbnb
Still the dominant platform for most Chicago hosts. Airbnb brings the highest volume of leisure travelers, particularly for urban apartments and design-forward properties. The platform's search algorithm rewards hosts with high review counts, Superhost status, and strong response rates.

**Best for:** City apartments, design-forward spaces, leisure travelers, group trips, bachelorette weekends.

### VRBO
VRBO skews toward families and groups booking entire homes. In Chicago, it performs best for larger units — two-bedrooms and up — particularly in neighborhoods like Lincoln Park, Wicker Park, and Lakeview. VRBO travelers tend to book further in advance and stay longer.

**Best for:** Larger units, family travel, longer stays.

### Booking.com
Often overlooked by U.S. hosts, Booking.com's strength is international travelers and corporate guests. If your property is well-positioned for O'Hare or Midway, or near McCormick Place for convention season, Booking.com can fill a meaningful share of your calendar.

**Best for:** Convention season, international travelers, airport-adjacent properties.

## Why Multi-Channel Distribution Matters

Single-platform listing means single-platform risk. Platform algorithm changes, fee structure updates, and policy shifts can significantly impact your visibility overnight.

Multi-channel distribution also creates calendar competition — the same dates visible on multiple platforms creates a sense of urgency and can reduce lead time on bookings.

## The Management Overhead Problem

The reason most hosts don't list on multiple platforms: managing it manually is genuinely hard. Syncing calendars, managing different guest communication standards, and maintaining consistent pricing across platforms requires real infrastructure.
